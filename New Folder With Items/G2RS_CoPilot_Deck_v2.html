STEP 4
PROBLEM FRAMING

Framing iterations

The problem framing went through multiple iterations before arriving at the correct business frame.

Iteration 1 — Rejected

"Too many alerts"

Leads to reducing volume, improving precision. But even 100% precision generates alerts. Investigation time untouched.

Iteration 2 — Partial

"G2RS growth depends on customer capacity"

Correct direction but frames it as G2RS's problem. Wrong ownership framing — it's the customer's scalability gap.

Iteration 3 — Final

"G2RS gives detection. Not scalability."

As portfolios grow, violation queues grow. Risk teams can't scale linearly. G2RS must give customers a way to handle what it finds.

PM lesson: The framing determines the solution space. "Too many alerts" leads to filters. "No scalability" leads to workflow transformation.

Why not precision?

Even 100% precision generates alerts. Every true positive still requires full investigation. Doesn't touch the bottleneck.

Why not tiers alone?

Segment by risk: High → continuous monitoring; Medium → monthly, batched weekly; Low → quarterly, significant only. If 60–70% are low risk, volume drops at source.

Why strong: Reduces alerts at source. No workflow change, no adoption friction. Respects data constraint. Deploy quickly. Demand suppression: "Low-risk portfolio = minimal alerts" → customers send more merchants → G2RS unlocks growth.

Shortfalls: (1) Volume down, investigation time unchanged — each alert still 40 min. (2) Miscategorization risk → missed violations, regulatory exposure. (3) No learning system; tiering is static. (4) No workflow evolution for API/UI.

Deploy alongside Co-Pilot, not instead.

Why Co-Pilot?

Attacks the root cause directly. Scales as software. Near-zero marginal cost. Works within the data constraint.

Tradeoff: Tiers: ✓ Solve volume elegantly, ✓ Fast adoption — ✗ Investigation time unchanged, ✗ Tiering risk, ✗ No compounding value. Co-Pilot: ✓ Volume + investigation, ✓ Workflow transformation, ✓ Learning system — ✗ Behavioral change.

Strategic insight: Complementary layers — Tiers reduce incoming volume; Co-Pilot maximizes efficiency. Together: fewer alerts × faster investigation = full-system scalability.

Recommended: (1) Launch Co-Pilot first → prove efficiency → baseline metrics. (2) Add risk-based tiers → reduce volume → amplify Co-Pilot impact.

STEP 6 · THE SOLUTION
4

Layers of intelligence

Group → Guide → Assemble → Summarize

SOLUTION ARCHITECTURE

Four layers, one workflow

1

Violation-Type Grouping

Foundation layer

All gambling cases together, then prescription, then counterfeit. Analyst stays in the same mental model across the entire batch. Context loads once.

2

Investigation Checklist

Guidance layer

Pre-defined for each violation type: exactly which internal data to verify and why. Analyst executes the investigation — doesn't invent it.

3

Evidence Pack

Context layer

Compass Score, risk graph, merchant history, behavioral delta — assembled automatically on case open. Zero tool-switching.

4

AI Case Summary

Intelligence layer

Plain-language paragraph: who is the merchant, what was detected, what the pattern suggests. On-demand to prevent anchoring bias.

Constraint respected throughout: The checklist tells analysts what to verify internally — the Co-Pilot never pulls customer data. Decisions remain with the customer.

What's IN the MVP

5 features that deliver the complete investigation workflow — from case open to case close. Nothing less, nothing more.

What's NOT in the MVP

Analytics dashboard, bulk actions, team workflows, API integrations. Valuable — but they amplify; they don't transform.

Scoping principle

Ship the smallest thing that delivers the full transformation. Adding a new violation type template costs near-zero.
