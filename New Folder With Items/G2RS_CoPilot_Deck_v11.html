SOLUTION SPACE

Three credible paths. One recommendation.

REJECTED

Automate Investigation

Connect to bank's internal systems and run checks automatically.

Fails: Security boundary. Banks won't allow third-party access. Customer is legally liable for decisions.

COMPLEMENTARY

Risk-Based Tiers

High → continuous, Medium → monthly, Low → quarterly. Reduces volume at source.

Gap: Reduces count, not per-case effort. Miscategorization risk. No workflow improvement.

CHOSEN

Merchant Risk Co-Pilot

Workflow layer between detection and decision. Attacks root cause.

Wins: Reduces per-case time. Scales as software. Respects data boundary.

Tiers are complementary. Deploy as a second layer once the Co-Pilot proves workflow efficiency.

THE BIG IDEA

Group by type.
Load context once.
Work the batch.

The analyst becomes a violation-type expert for the duration of the batch — instead of a generalist starting cold on every alert.

SOLUTION BREAKDOWN

Four layers. Each one compounds.

LAYER 1 — FOUNDATION

Violation-type grouping

47 random alerts → 3 structured batches. Eliminates context switching entirely.

LAYER 2

Evidence pack

Merchant history, Compass Score, EverC risk signals — pre-assembled. No 3-tool lookup.

LAYER 3

Verification checklist

Exactly what internal data to check for this violation type. No guessing.

LAYER 4

AI case summary

On-demand, plain language: who is this merchant, what's the pattern. 20 seconds.

RISKS & MITIGATIONS

Known risks. Planned responses.

Low adoption — analysts resist new workflow.

Phase 0 shadows real analysts. Co-design with 2–3 partners. Measure adoption weekly.

AI hallucination — case summary generates wrong context.

AI is advisory only. Analyst always verifies. Summary is click-to-reveal, not default. Audit trail on every edit.

Grouping logic errors — violations mis-categorized.

Grouping uses G2RS's existing violation taxonomy. Analyst can override. Feedback loop corrects.

Data boundary breach — customer fears we access their data.

Architecture ensures zero access to internal data. Checklist tells analyst what to check — never accesses it directly.

Tiers needed first — volume too high even with grouping.

Co-Pilot first proves workflow. Tiers deploy as second layer to reduce funnel input. Sequence matters.

Without the Co-Pilot, G2RS growth is capped by its customers' analyst capacity.

With it, a customer with 100,000 merchants can monitor all of them — and ask G2RS to send more.

That's what scalability means.
