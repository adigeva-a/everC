THE DIAGNOSIS

This is not a volume problem.

It is a structure problem.

G2RS gives customers detection. It doesn't give them a way to act on it at scale.

Fix the structure → solve the scale.

ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED

Three directions. One chosen.

Rejected A: Better Detection Precision

Even 100% precision still generates alerts. Every true positive still needs investigation. Doesn't touch investigation time.

Complementary B: Risk-Based Monitoring Tiers

Segment the portfolio into dynamic risk tiers instead of monitoring all merchants equally:

  • High risk → continuous deep monitoring, all violations surfaced
  • Medium risk → monthly monitoring, violations batched weekly
  • Low risk → quarterly monitoring, only significant violations surfaced

If 60–70% of merchants are genuinely low risk, this dramatically reduces violation volume at the source.

Why strong: Directly reduces alert volume at the source. No workflow or behavior change required. No adoption friction. Fully respects the internal data constraint. Can be deployed quickly. Demand suppression: A customer afraid to send 50,000 merchants hears: "Your low-risk portfolio will generate minimal alerts." → They send more merchants → G2RS unlocks growth.

Where it falls short: (1) Reduces volume — but not investigation time. Each surfaced violation still requires the same manual investigation. (2) Miscategorization risk: incorrect tiering = missed violations for months → regulatory exposure. (3) No learning system; tiering is largely static. (4) Does not evolve the workflow — API and UI customers experience no change in investigation process.

Chosen ✓ C: Merchant Risk Co-Pilot

Attacks the root cause: structural mismatch between how alerts arrive and how investigations run. Scales as software.

Tradeoff: Risk-based tiers: ✓ Solve volume elegantly, ✓ Fast low-risk adoption — ✗ Leave investigation time unchanged, ✗ Introduce tiering risk, ✗ Do not compound in value. Co-Pilot: ✓ Solves both volume and investigation time, ✓ Improves workflow fundamentally, ✓ Builds a learning system — ✗ Requires behavioral change.

Strategic insight: These are complementary layers — Risk-based tiers reduce incoming volume; Co-Pilot maximizes efficiency of remaining investigations. Together: fewer alerts × faster investigation = full-system scalability.

Recommended approach: (1) Launch Co-Pilot first → prove investigation efficiency gains → establish baseline metrics. (2) Introduce risk-based monitoring tiers as a second layer → reduce incoming volume → amplify Co-Pilot impact.
