CONTEXT

G2RS detects merchant violations

Gambling, counterfeit goods, prescription drugs, IP infringement — across merchant portfolios for banks, fintechs, and payment facilitators.

EverC Scanning

Deep web scanning, Compass Score, risk graph, behavioral signals

Violation Alerts

Findings sent to customers with >90% precision on detection

Customer Investigation

Risk analysts must investigate every finding before taking action

Key constraint: G2RS cannot access customer transaction data. All intelligence comes from external scanning only.

THE ASSIGNMENT

"Solve the operational overhead caused by large numbers of detected violations reaching the customer."

The first instinct: too many alerts = too much work. Reduce alerts, improve precision, triage harder.

That framing leads to the wrong solutions

WHY IT MATTERS

G2RS gives customers detection.

It does not give them scalability.

Existing customers self-limit

They stop expanding portfolios because they've hit analyst capacity. G2RS revenue growth plateaus with them.

Prospects don't adopt

They do the math — 50K merchants × violation rate × investigation time — and the workload is unmanageable. Deals don't close.

ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED

Three paths evaluated

Rejected

Improve detection precision

Push from >90% toward 99%. Even 100% precision still generates alerts. Every true positive still requires investigation. Doesn't touch investigation time.

Complementary

Risk-based monitoring tiers

Segment by risk, screen less often. Reduces upstream volume but doesn't fix what happens when alerts arrive. Deploy alongside, not instead.

Chosen

Merchant Risk Co-Pilot

Attacks the root cause — the structural mismatch. Scales as software. Works within the data constraint. Near-zero cost per new violation type.
