THE SCALABILITY GAP

G2RS gives customers detection. It doesn't give them scalability.

Customer's merchant portfolio ↑ → Violation volume ↑ → Investigation time × volume → Customer's team capacity = FIXED

G2RS has no scalability layer for what happens after detection. Every true positive becomes a unit of work the customer cannot absorb at scale.

Existing customers cap their portfolio at analyst capacity

Prospective customers don't adopt — the math doesn't work for them

ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED

Three paths. Here's how I evaluated them.

REJECTED

Automate the investigation — connect to bank's internal systems

Run checks automatically using customer's transaction logs, chargebacks, and invoices.

Why rejected: Security boundary. Banks won't allow third-party vendor access to internal investigation data. The customer is legally liable for risk decisions.

COMPLEMENTARY

Risk-Based Monitoring Tiers

Segment portfolio: High → continuous, Medium → monthly, Low → quarterly. If 60–70% of merchants are low risk, this reduces volume at the source.

Why it's strong:

Reduces alert volume · No behavior change · No adoption friction · Respects data constraint · Deploys quickly

Where it falls short:

Doesn't reduce per-case effort · Miscategorization → regulatory risk · Static tiering, no learning · No workflow improvement

Complementary, not rejected. Best deployed alongside the Co-Pilot as a second layer.

CHOSEN ✓

Merchant Risk Co-Pilot

A workflow layer that transforms how customers consume violation findings. Attacks the root cause — the structural mismatch between how alerts arrive and how investigations run. Scales as software. Works within the data constraint.

The investigation for a gambling violation is essentially the same investigation every time. The merchant changes. The process doesn't. But the analyst starts from zero every single time.

Alerts arrive ungrouped. Investigation is violation-type-specific.

That mismatch is the bottleneck.

1. No grouping by violation type → context switching after every case

2. No violation-type checklist → analyst rebuilds guidance from scratch

3. No pre-loaded context → merchant story reconstructed manually every time

This is why G2RS customers can't scale. Not their team size — the structure of how alerts arrive.

THE SOLUTION

The scalability layer G2RS is missing.

Not a new detection product. The layer between detection and decision.

Violation detected → Grouped by type → Evidence assembled → Analyst verifies → Decision + audit trail

G2RS cannot access customer transaction data. The Co-Pilot is designed around this — it tells analysts exactly what to verify internally, without crossing that line.

What changes:

Investigation is structured, Context is pre-loaded, Checklist is violation-type specific

What stays the same:

Analyst makes the decision, Internal data stays internal, Customer remains liable
